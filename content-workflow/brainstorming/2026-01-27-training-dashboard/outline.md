# Outline: Why I Built My Own Running Dashboard

## Hook
Open with a relatable scenario: Your watch buzzes. "Congratulations on your fastest 5K this month!" But you don't feel faster. And last month, you had three "fastest" runs that contradicted each other.

## The Problem
- Fitness apps celebrate individual achievements
- Weekly summaries create false sense of progress or failure
- Premium features lock away historical comparisons
- You own the data but can't see it in context

## What I Wanted
- See if structured training (Tactic Method) actually works
- Compare training blocks across months, not just weeks
- Own my data without paying for insights about my own body
- Answer: Am I actually improving?

## What I Built
Brief overview (not technical tutorial):
- Downloads Garmin FIT files automatically
- Tracks: time, distance, pace, heart rate across runs
- Visualizes trends over months with run/walk intervals
- Shows training events and phases

## What I Learned

### 1. Numbers Without Context Are Noise
Single run metrics mean nothing. Your "slow" run last Tuesday might be faster than any run you did six months ago.

### 2. Week-to-Week Progress Is a Myth
Weekly summaries hide the truth. Training shows results over months. The data revealed:
- Three weeks with no improvement
- Sudden jump after rest week
- Plateau that broke after changing training structure

### 3. Structured Training Shows Up in Data
When I started following a structured program:
- Distance trends changed slope
- Run/walk interval splits became more consistent
- Heart rate at same pace dropped over time

Example: Show before/after trend lines

### 4. Seeing Accumulation Keeps You Going
The most surprising benefit wasn't seeing trends. It was seeing the pile of work already done.

When energy is low and motivation fades, the dashboard shows months of effort. Not as a streak to maintain or a goal to chase, but as evidence: you've already done this much. You can keep going.

Apps celebrate streaks. This celebrates accumulation. The difference matters when you need a push.

### 5. You Can Trust the Long Game
Apps want you to check daily. Real fitness happens slowly. Monthly view shows what daily anxiety misses.

## How You Can Do This

Not a full tutorial, but guidance:

1. **Get your data**: Garmin lets you download FIT files (explain briefly)
2. **Choose your tools**: I used Python + React because I'm comfortable with data analysis, but tools don't matter as much as seeing patterns. AI tools like Claude or GitHub Copilot can help you understand and modify code.
3. **Start simple**: Track one metric that matters to you
4. **Be patient**: You need months of data to see meaningful trends
5. **Use AI assistance**: Don't let code intimidate you - Claude, Copilot, and similar tools can explain code, suggest changes, and troubleshoot errors

GitHub repo available as starting point to customize.

## The Real Insight

Fitness apps are designed to keep you engaged daily. Your body improves monthly.

The best training insight isn't from one run. It's from seeing six months of context that reveals whether your effort is actually working.

And when motivation dips, it's not another badge you need. It's seeing the work you've already accumulated. That's what keeps you going.
