# Scientific Sources Verification

## ✅ All Sources Verified with Primary Research Papers

### Papers Used in Post

#### 1. Taylor-Haas et al. (2022) - Leg Length & Speed Predict 50% of Cadence
- **Full Citation:** Taylor-Haas, J. A., Garcia, M. C., Rauh, M. J., Peel, S., Paterno, M. V., Bazett-Jones, D. M., Ford, K. R., & Long, J. T. (2022). Cadence in youth long-distance runners is predicted by leg length and running speed. *Gait & Posture*, 98, 266-270.
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.gaitpost.2022.09.085
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36209689/
- **Key Finding:** 48-55% of cadence variance explained by leg length and running speed
- **Study:** 138 youth long-distance runners
- **Used for:** Individual variation claim, formula

#### 2. Burns et al. (2019) - Elite Ultramarathon Cadence Range 155-203 spm
- **Full Citation:** Burns, G. T., Zendler, J. D., & Zernicke, R. F. (2019). Step frequency patterns of elite ultramarathon runners during a 100-km road race. *Journal of Applied Physiology*, 126(2), 462-468.
- **DOI:** 10.1152/japplphysiol.00374.2018
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30543498/
- **Key Finding:** Elite runners at 100-km World Championship had cadences from 155-203 spm (avg 182)
- **Study:** Top 25 male and female finishers
- **Used for:** Elite runner variation, debunking 180 myth

#### 3. Leacox et al. (2025) - Running Speed Effects on Cadence
- **Full Citation:** Leacox, A., Fashingbauer, L., Ferguson, T., Zajakowski, A., Baum, B., & Reinking, M. (2025). The Effect of Running Speed on Cadence and Running Kinetics. *International Journal of Sports Physical Therapy*, 20(7), 957-963.
- **DOI:** 10.26603/001c.140544
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40620407/
- **PMC:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12222555/
- **Key Finding:** First study using experienced runners to examine speed effects on both cadence and ground reaction forces
- **Study:** 30 experienced runners (ages 21-49, running 24+ km/week)
- **Used for:** Speed-cadence relationship

#### 4. Figueiredo et al. (2025) - Cadence & Injury Prevention Systematic Review
- **Full Citation:** Figueiredo, I., Silva, M. R., & Sousa, J. E. (2025). The Influence of Running Cadence on Biomechanics and Injury Prevention: A Systematic Review. *Cureus*, 17(8), e90322.
- **DOI:** 10.7759/cureus.90322
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40964543/
- **PMC:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12440572/
- **Key Finding:** Lower cadence increases ground reaction forces and injury risk
- **Study:** Systematic review
- **Used for:** Injury risk at very low cadence

#### 5. Ibe et al. (2024) - Running Economy & Cadence Changes
- **Full Citation:** Ibe, O., Musci, R. V., & Roper, J. L. (2024). Running Economy Changes Alter Predicted Running Speed and Performance in Collegiate Runners. *International Journal of Exercise Science*, 17(1), 965-974.
- **DOI:** 10.70252/GLKW8080
- **PubMed:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39253097/
- **PMC:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11382781/
- **Key Finding:** Running economy changes don't significantly alter predicted performance
- **Study:** Collegiate runners
- **Used for:** Forced cadence changes don't help economy
